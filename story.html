How it all began

Hannah's Story

✿

Once upon last summer, I had the crazy idea to go on a date a week. I have no idea why I thought it would be a good idea... It was probably some of God's handiwork. Not knowing many single men my age, I had no choice but to use dating apps (for the sole purpose of accomplishing my goal).

I had the hope that going on so many dates would make me like dating more, and less awkward on dates.

Somehow, thanks to mutual and to great friends who set me up, I accomplished this goal. Major shoutout to my little sister Bethany, who swiped on my mutual account every Sunday and messaged people on my behalf 🙃

When school started, I was so tired of dating. I had gone on so many dates that felt like dead ends, and I didn't have the time for my goal. So, I paused my account. But... I had a few people left who Bethany had swiped on, and didn't want to be that person to ghost them. So, I told myself I'd go on dates with them if they asked me soon, and then be done. And I was SO excited for no more mutual dates!

I remember when I was going on my first date with Adam. I told my roommates I had a date, and that it was my last one for my date-a-week goal. One of my roommates knew Adam already from a math class!

The day of the date, I was coming straight from my temple shift. I was famished, so changed and ate dinner and then headed over to the park where we were meeting... I was 20 minutes late!

Somehow, Adam didn't judge me too much for that! He also didn't judge me for my lack of frisbee golf skills!

We just clicked on that first date, and Adam says that he could tell I was REALLY happy when he asked me on a second :)

Adam's Story

✿

After going on many dates with people he wasn't interested in, Adam felt a prompting that he needed to be less prideful and download a dating app to expand his options. After about a month of resisting that feeling, he finally gave in — but made some firm rules: Mutual only in the mornings or evenings, never longer than fifteen minutes a day. His plan was to go on lots of short first dates to figure out what kind of person he was looking for. In his mind, dating worked the same way missionaries find people to teach — and he used all the same techniques, where applicable.

Life got busy fast, and Mutual worked... perhaps too well. Soon Adam was going on several dates a week, so he made a new rule: ask only one girl per week. When he first came across Hannah's profile, he didn't act on it — he simply didn't have room for another date that week. A week passed, and when he opened the app again, Hannah appeared once more. He was about to swipe down when a distinct thought came to him: "What if she is supposed to be your wife?" He sat with that for a moment, then swiped up.

Being pressed for time, Adam planned his usual low-effort first date: frisbee golf at Rock Canyon — one hour, no planning required. Another girl had asked him out for later that same evening, and Adam had already committed. When he woke up that morning swamped with things to do, he seriously weighed canceling one of the dates. He wanted to cancel Hannah's — not out of anything against her, but purely out of stress — but couldn't bring himself to do it. It felt rude.

Hannah was twenty minutes late. Adam did homework while he waited, which helped, but in his mind she had already struck out. Then he saw her — and his breath caught. It was a fleeting feeling, but it was real, and for Adam, that had never happened when meeting someone for the first time.

On the date, Hannah put him surprisingly at ease. He was impressed by her spirituality and capability — two things he always looked for. When Adam wrapped up the date after forty minutes, he felt bad that she had walked all the way to the top of the course, so he drove her back to her car. In that brief drive, he could tell she had genuinely enjoyed their time together. Despite his own rule against asking for a second date on a first, he asked for her number. Hannah quickly assured him she was terrible at texting — but that she'd be fast to respond to him. Adam smiled and drove away. He was late to his next date, and by the end of that evening told that girl they wouldn't be going out again.

But Hannah still wasn't in the clear. Adam nearly ended things — until he met her family. He was struck by their goodness and by the way Hannah loved each of them. It was exactly the kind of home he hoped to build someday, and for the first time, he began seriously considering her.

There was still one more close call. Another girl he'd been seeing was at roughly the same level of interest in his mind, and continuing to date two people at once felt wrong to him. He was close to walking away from Hannah entirely — until his friend Eric told him that was a terrible idea, and reminded him that the whole point of dating was to get to know people better. We all owe Eric a debt of gratitude. A week later, Adam stopped seeing anyone else and turned his full attention to one question: was Hannah the one?

On their next date, Hannah said exactly the thing Adam hadn't been able to articulate — the thing that had quietly been holding him back. It felt like an answer to a prayer. Not long after, he received a clear answer from God that he should pursue Hannah — and that was that.
